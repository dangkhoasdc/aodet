To compensate the lack of features, let me share an idea, which I think very interesting.

A long time ago, I found a data structure named Zero-suppressed decision diagram(Wiki). It is an improvement of BDD - binary decision diagram. You may wonder what the hell this is. But did you know that Knuth mentioned ZDD is the most fascinated data structure in his recent memory? Yes, it is from the father of analysis of algorithm. It must be a big deal (Source).

Now, ZDD or BDD is a way to represent a set or a family of set, that covers many computer science problems, especially in discrete constrained problems, namely searching, optimization and representation.

Imagine that you are able to represent a feature representation by a binary string (image hashing) and then apply ZDD/BDD to that representation. Eventually, you are able to retrieve, search, optimize directly on ZDD/BDD without decompression and in compact structure. How cool is that?
